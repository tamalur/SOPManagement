Purpose:

The purpose of Radiant’s SOP Management System is to define the requirements for establishing, maintaining and controlling SOPs to include changes (additions, revision, deletions) to SOPs.

SOP Identification and Control

SOPs within Radiant’s SOP Management tool are designated as controlled documents and are not to be distributed outside the company without the approval of the respective owner of the SOP.

·         Only controlled copies of documents shall be used.

·         If copies of a procedure are required on the floor to complete an activity, a copy must be printed from the system. This copy will be deemed uncontrolled once printed and must be discarded when no longer in use.

·         Obsolete documents shall be removed from access by employees and removed from use if physically printed. This activity is done electronically by archiving documents and placing them in restricted access.

·         SOPS are identified by an alpha-numeric identifier and revision status (ABC-12). For example, for Quality Assurance & Regulatory Affairs for food grade SOPs, the below is the naming convention: QAG-01

·         The revision level of the first or new SOP is 1 and the next revision level will be 2.

 

SOP Format:

The following template is applicable to all SOPs and each SOP should have at a minimum the below-mentioned sections:

SOP Section

Description

Title

Name of the SOP

SOP #:

SOP number

Revision #:

The first version will be “1” of a SOP.

Effective Date:

The date identified as the “effective date” of the instructions/details outlined.

Where required (e.g. GMP, AIB), all training must be completed prior to the effective date of the document issued.

Owner:

SOP owners are identified based on the ownership of the process.

Reviewers:

SOP reviewers are identified based on the business process and impact to department.

Approver:

SOP approvers are identified based on the business process and impact to department.

Approval Date:

The date on which responsible personnel have approved the document.

Purpose:

An introductory paragraph to state the document’s purpose or intent.

Scope:

The scope is any combination of tasks, time, phase or work definition. The scope may be omitted if the scope information is logically included in the purpose.

Equipment:

The equipment used in performing the task, if applicable.

Definitions:

A section defining document-specific jargon, words and acronyms that may assist in a better understanding of the procedure.

Responsibility:

Outlines the individual and/or department responsible for the procedure and the specific activity they perform.

Policy:

The steps required to carry out the procedure.

Headings and sub-headings are used where applicable to effectively map the flow of information. A process flow diagram may be used as documentation of the methodology.

The amount of detail included must be determined based on the type of activity, the complexity of the process, its relationship to other processes and the competence of personnel performing the tasks.

References:

A list of reference documents that will enable the user to complete the instructions outlined or provide more detail to the requirements.

Revision History:

Includes a brief summary of changes.
